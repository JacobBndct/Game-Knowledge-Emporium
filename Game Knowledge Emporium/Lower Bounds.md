$A \subseteq \Bbb R$ is called *bounded below* if $$\exists \; \ell \in \Bbb R \;\;\; \text{such that} \;\;\; \ell \le a \;\;\; \forall \; a \in A$$
## Example
$$A = \{ {1 \over n}: n \in \Bbb N\} = \{ 1, {1 \over 2}, {1 \over 3},\dots \}$$
Any number $\ell \le 0$ is an lower bound for $A$
