$A \subseteq \Bbb R$ us called *bounded above* if $$\exists \; b \in \Bbb R \;\;\; \text{such that} a \le b \;\;\;\forall \; a \in A$$ 
## Example
$$A = \{ {1 \over n}: n \in \Bbb N\} = \{ 1, {1 \over 2}, {1 \over 3},\dots \}$$
Any number $b \ge 1$ is an upper bound for $A$
